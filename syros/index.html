Syros

studied

A time-sliced reconstruction of Syros in which the analytical spine is settlement displacement as a proxy for Aegean pacification.

The thesis the slider narrates

When the seas are contested by raiding and piracy, people live up and inland — the Early Cycladic Kastri citadel in the north, then the Venetian-Catholic hill-town of Ano Syros above the harbour. When the seas are pacified, settlement descends to an open coast — the ancient shore polis, and finally Ermoupolis, the refugee-founded 1820s port that became the 19th-century capital of the Cyclades. Drag the year slider: the colour of where people dared to live moves coast → hill → coast.

How to read it

  • Points are filtered by the slider year and coloured by founding driver — this field is the thesis.
  • Two point-events carry the pirate/hero, sea-as-contested reading: 1537 (Barbarossa takes Syros) and the Grammata mariners' votives.
  • The frame card shows how the island was named & charted in each period — the cartographic record layered on top of the settlement record.

Scope of this build. This is the fully-scriptable analytical spine: the settlement time-series and the naming/cartographic timeline. The historical raster layers are deliberately not included yet, per the brief’s two hard rules — pre-modern isolarios (Buondelmonti, Sonetti, Camocio) must be shown unwarped, and only the 18–19c planimetric Admiralty charts get georeferenced, whose GCP selection is a manual step. The frame card cites each source and links to OldMapsOnline so those layers can be dropped in next.
